## About the Book

Thomas Hale has built a life on being agreeable. He's attractive in an effortless way, calm under pressure, and unusually attentive—someone people lean toward at parties and lean on in crises. When someone confides in him, they leave feeling lighter. Thomas leaves with something else: **leverage**.

After a highly public "accident" shocks the community, two more tragedies follow—different victims, different circumstances, the same clean conclusion: nobody to blame. While social media mourns and moves on, **DI Amina Rahman** can't ignore the unease that lingers between the facts. The deaths are too neat. The narratives too ready-made. And in the background of every aftermath stands the same reassuring figure: Thomas—present, helpful, and somehow indispensable.

Amina's investigation becomes a race against something she can't photograph or fingerprint: **influence**. Because Thomas doesn't destroy people with chaos. He does it with persuasion, secrets, and the soft power of being believed. The closer Amina gets, the more she risks becoming the only person who looks paranoid in a city that adores him.

## What Makes This Book Different

_The Man Everyone Likes_ is not just another crime thriller. It's a deep psychological exploration of:

- **The Violence of Charm** — How likability can be weaponized
- **The Fragility of Reputation** — When perception becomes reality
- **Social Manipulation** — The soft power of being believed
- **Modern Investigation** — Racing against influence rather than evidence

This is a story about the most dangerous kind of monster: the one you'd invite into your home.

If you enjoyed psychological thrillers like _You_, _The Talented Mr. Ripley_, or _Gone Girl_, this book will pull you into a world where:

- Every smile hides calculation
- Every act of kindness serves a purpose
- The most dangerous weapon is trust itself

**Perfect for readers who love:**
